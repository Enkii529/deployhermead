# AI News Briefing - May 16, 2026

**1. Anthropic CFO says AI now writes 90% of its code, changing white-collar jobs**

Krishna Rao of Anthropic disclosed that artificial intelligence now writes 90% of the company's code, shifting white-collar work from execution to oversight. This revelation highlights how deeply AI is being integrated into core software development processes and the resulting transformation of job roles.

**2. Microsoft intensifies AI with $12B OpenAI stake and near-continuous trading**

Microsoft's $12 billion investment in OpenAI over seven years now gives it a 27% stake. The tech giant is also advancing AI-driven trading systems that operate almost round the clock, signaling aggressive expansion in financial technology applications.

**3. arXiv cracks down on AI-generated “slop” with yearlong bans**

The popular preprint server arXiv announced it will ban submitters for a year if they include AI-generated hallucinations, fake citations, or unedited content. This decisive action aims to preserve the integrity of scientific literature amid rising concerns about AI-generated academic spam.

**4. Floating AI data centers proposed as sustainable solution**

Startup Panthalassa raised $140 million to build floating AI data centers at sea. The concept uses ocean waves for power and seawater for cooling, potentially reducing the environmental footprint of compute-intensive AI operations.

**5. Majority of US doctors use AI medical tool, patients largely unaware**

A survey revealed that nearly all US doctors quietly use OpenEvidence, an AI-powered medical search tool, while most patients remain unaware of its use. This gap between clinical adoption and patient transparency raises important ethical questions.

**6. AI agents reshape SaaS and enterprise workflows**

Recent reports show AI agents are turning SaaS applications into headless, deterministic engines, enabling governed and automated enterprise workflows. Meanwhile, 31% of leaders are now framing AI agents as "employees" and adding them to org charts, signaling a shift in how organizations conceptualize AI workforce integration.
